TEA DISEASE

Tea Leaf Rust (Hemileia vastatrix)

Symptoms: Orange to rusty-brown powdery spots on the upper surface of leaves, resulting in defoliation and reduced vigor. Leaves may become distorted and drop prematurely.
Causes: Fungal infection spread by wind or rain. Thrives in warm and humid conditions.
Treatment: Regular pruning to improve air circulation, fungicide application, removal and destruction of infected leaves, and proper sanitation practices.

Tea Anthracnose (Colletotrichum spp.)

Symptoms: Brown to black circular lesions on leaves, stems, and buds. Lesions may have yellowish halos. Infected leaves may wither and drop.
Causes: Fungal infection favored by high humidity, prolonged leaf wetness, and poor sanitation.
Treatment: Pruning to increase air circulation, fungicide application, removal and destruction of infected plant parts, and improvement of cultural practices.

Tea Brown Blight (Exobasidium vexans)

Symptoms: White to light brown blister-like spots on the lower leaf surface, which may turn dark brown or black with age. Leaves may become distorted and curl.
Causes: Fungal infection facilitated by cool, wet conditions and poor sanitation.
Treatment: Removal and destruction of infected leaves, pruning for better airflow, copper-based fungicide application, and improvement of sanitation practices.

Tea Mosquito Bug (Helopeltis spp.)

Symptoms: Pinhead-sized, yellowish-brown, or blackish-brown bugs feeding on young shoots and leaves, causing damage to buds and leaves. Affected leaves may show browning, drying, and distortion.
Causes: Infestation by mosquito bugs, especially during dry periods.
Treatment: Mechanical removal of bugs, regular monitoring and scouting, use of insecticides when necessary, and maintenance of proper hygiene in tea gardens.

Tea Red Spider Mite (Oligonychus coffeae)

Symptoms: Tiny yellowish or reddish mites on the underside of leaves, causing stippling and yellowing of foliage. Severe infestation leads to leaf curling, drying, and premature defoliation.
Causes: Infestation by red spider mites, favored by hot and dry conditions.
Treatment: Use of predatory mites or beneficial insects, application of acaricides, maintaining proper moisture levels, and improving overall garden hygiene.
